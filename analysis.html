1.1 float velocity

  • K. Lebedev, H. Yoshinari, N. A. Maximenko, and P. W. Hacker. YoMaHa’07: Velocity data assessed from trajectories of Argo floats at parking level and at the sea surface, IPRC Technical Note No. 4(2), June 12, 2007, 16p.
    • derive Argo drift speed at park depth to quantify ocean circulation at 1,000 m depth
    • See also, Katsumata, K., Yoshinari, H. Uncertainties in global mapping of Argo drift data at the parking level. J Oceanogr 66, 553–569 (2010). https://doi.org/10.1007/s10872-010-0046-4
    • Estimate parking level (1000m) zonal (eastward \(u\)) and meridional (northward \(v\)) velocity components using the coordinates (\([X,Y]\) = [lon, lat]) of floats before it dives and after it re-arrives at the sea surface \[\begin{gather} (U_\text{deep}^n, V_\text{deep}^n) = ([X,Y]^n_\text{first} - [X,Y]^{n-1}_\text{last}) / (T^n_\text{first} - T^{n-1}_\text{last}) \end{gather}\]
    • Use a regression to estimate surface velocity and errors for deep/surface velocities
  • Above referenced in more recent literature, e.g., Oke, Peter R., Tatiana Rykova, Gabriela S. Pilo, and Jennifer L. Lovell. “Estimating Argo float trajectories under ice.” Earth and Space Science 9, no. 7 (2022): e2022EA002312.

2.2 velocity

  • velocity components u/v are calculated by finding the change in longitude/latitude and dividing by change in time.
  • geosphere::distVincentyEllipsoid, an ellipsoidal (WGS84 by default) great-circle-distance, is used to calculate the distance between float obs.
  • also calculated the magnitude of the velocity (speed) and the bearing of the velocity vectors in radians

2.4 correlation between temperature and velocity

  • correlation between temperatures and velocities for floats with multiple observations
  • dtemp_ variables are the change in temperature for consecutive float observations at the given depth
    • negligible correlations between change in temperature and velocity
  • small to moderate negative correlation between eastward velocity and temperature/vhc across depths in southern hemisphere
    • southern antarctic ocean which has positive correlation between eastward velocity and temperature/vhc
  • negligible negative correlation between eastward velocity and temperature/vhc across depths in northern hemisphere
    • artic ocean has larger correlations but few obs
  • negligible or no correlation between northward velocity and temperature
