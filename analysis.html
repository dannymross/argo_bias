1 literature

1.1 float velocity

  • K. Lebedev, H. Yoshinari, N. A. Maximenko, and P. W. Hacker. YoMaHa’07: Velocity data assessed from trajectories of Argo floats at parking level and at the sea surface, IPRC Technical Note No. 4(2), June 12, 2007, 16p.
    • derive Argo drift speed at park depth to quantify ocean circulation at 1,000 m depth
    • See also, Katsumata, K., Yoshinari, H. Uncertainties in global mapping of Argo drift data at the parking level. J Oceanogr 66, 553–569 (2010). https://doi.org/10.1007/s10872-010-0046-4
    • Estimate parking level (1000m) zonal (eastward \(u\)) and meridional (northward \(v\)) velocity components using the coordinates (\([X,Y]\)) of floats before it dives and after it re-arrives at the sea surface \[\begin{gather} (U_\text{deep}^n, V_\text{deep}^n) = ([X,Y]^n_\text{first} - [X,Y]^{n-1}_\text{last}) / (T^n_\text{first} - T^{n-1}_\text{last}) \end{gather}\]
    • Use a regression to estimate surface velocity and errors for deep/surface velocities

2.2 velocity

velocity components u/v are calculated by finding the change in longitude/latitude and dividing by change in time. geosphere::distVincentyEllipsoid is used to calculate the change in distance.

also calculated the magnitude of the velocity (speed) and the bearing of the velocity vectors in radians
